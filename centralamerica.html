Central America

Cuba

I’d been curious about Cuba for a long time, so it was really exciting to touch down in Havana on a late December evening. My hosts were a lovely couple - a psychologist and a dental technician.

Highlights of my two weeks in Cuba included a few days in Vinales, an evening at the Fábrica de Arte Cubano, and a visit to Radio Havana (my visit inspired by the classic Rancid track).

At Radio Havana, my friend and I were mistaken for American experts on radical Islam and were very nearly ushered into an interview, before explaining in our broken Spanish that we simply wanted to check out the studio.

Guatemala

Guatemala was beautiful. From ruins to rivers and colonial architecture to Lake Atitlan, I loved every moment.

Hiking around the stunning lake and waking up each morning to wander 50 metres to enjoy an outrageous view of the volcanoes made it very difficult to leave and continue my journey south to El Salvador and Nicaragua.

Nicaragua

An interest in the Nicaraguan Revolution and the role of the Sandinistas is what drew me to Nicaragua.

In Leon, I was fortunate to meet a veteran of the Revolution, and to be taken on a tour of a building of significance to the Sandinistas by this gentleman.

Leon is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage.
